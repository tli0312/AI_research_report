Company Overview

Berkshire Hathaway is the world's largest diversified conglomerate, built over six decades by Warren Buffett into a trillion-dollar enterprise. Effective January 1, 2026, Greg Abel assumed the role of President & CEO, with Buffett remaining as Chairman. The company operates through four major pillars — insurance, BNSF Railway, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and a vast portfolio of manufacturing, service, and retail businesses — while maintaining one of the largest public equity portfolios in the world.

Recent Underperformance Context

BRK-B's flat 1-year return vs. the S&P 500's 18.8% is attributable to the leadership transition period and Buffett's deliberate de-risking (massive equity sales building the $373B cash pile). This is a feature, not a bug — Berkshire is now positioned with more dry powder than any company in history. The 3-year and longer-term track records demonstrate consistent outperformance through full market cycles.

VI

Proposed Allocation Strategy

BRK-B is recommended as a core holding within a diversified portfolio. Its unique characteristics — conglomerate diversification, no dividend drag, tax-efficient compounding, and massive cash buffer — make it suitable for multiple portfolio roles:

Portfolio Role Suggested Allocation Rationale
Core Equity Replacement 10–20% of equity sleeve Substitutes for broad equity exposure with built-in diversification across sectors, plus embedded cash/bond allocation via the $373B treasury position.
Defensive Growth 5–10% of total portfolio Low-beta compounder with downside protection. No dividend tax drag. Historically lower drawdowns than the S&P 500.
Cash Alternative Tactical position 36% of market cap is in T-bills. Buying BRK-B at 1.5x book effectively buys Treasury yields plus equity upside on the operating businesses.

Tax Efficiency Advantage

Berkshire has never paid a dividend, meaning investors compound returns without annual tax drag. The company returns capital through buybacks (resumed March 2026) which are more tax-efficient. For taxable accounts, this structure can produce significantly better after-tax returns compared to dividend-paying alternatives.

Downside Floor Analysis

Cash and T-bills alone ($373B) represent ~$173/share in Class B terms. Adding the equity portfolio ($274B = ~$127/share) and insurance float value, the sum-of-the-parts floor is estimated at ~$400-420/share — implying only 12-16% downside from current levels in an extreme scenario. This asymmetric risk/reward profile is a key attraction.

VIII

Risk Factors

Med

Succession & Key-Man Risk

Buffett (94) remains Chairman but is no longer CEO. Greg Abel's track record is strong but unproven at the CEO level for a $1T conglomerate. Todd Combs' departure to JPMorgan leaves Ted Weschler as the sole investment manager. CFO Marc Hamburg retires June 2027.

Med

Equity Portfolio Concentration

Top 5 holdings represent 71% of the $274B equity portfolio. Apple alone is 22.6%. A material decline in Apple or American Express would significantly impact book value and sentiment.

Med

Cash Drag & Rate Sensitivity

$373B in T-bills generates attractive yield today, but rate cuts would reduce this income stream. If cash is not deployed into higher-return assets, it becomes a drag on overall ROE.

High

Insurance Catastrophe Exposure

Q4 2025 underwriting profits fell 54% YoY due to elevated catastrophe losses. Management indicated they will write less P&C business near-term. A major catastrophe event could produce significant losses.

Med

BHE Regulatory & Legal

PacifiCorp (BHE subsidiary) faces ongoing wildfire liability lawsuits. Clean energy tax credit phase-out under the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" threatens renewable energy returns.

Low

Size Constraints

At $1T+ market cap, the universe of needle-moving acquisitions is extremely limited. Berkshire needs larger and larger deals to meaningfully impact growth, narrowing the opportunity set.

Recent Strategic Actions Under Abel

January 1, 2026

Greg Abel becomes CEO. Buffett remains Chairman. Board unanimously approved the transition after Buffett's May 2025 announcement. Bylaws amended to formally separate Chairman and CEO roles for the first time.

January 2, 2026

OxyChem acquisition completed. $9.7B cash purchase of Occidental Petroleum's chemicals division. Top-3 U.S. producer of PVC and chlor-alkali. Largest deal in several years — signals continued appetite for industrial assets.

February 28, 2026

Abel's first shareholder letter. Emphasized continuity — disciplined investing, decentralized management, focus on "easily understandable, high-barrier, strong management" acquisition targets. Stated he will personally oversee the equity portfolio.

March 2026

Tokio Marine partnership. NICO investing $1.8B for 2.49% stake in Tokio Marine Holdings. Collaboration on reinsurance and global M&A — extending Berkshire's international insurance footprint.

March 2026

Share buybacks resumed. ~$200M repurchased — the first buyback since 2024. Abel signaled that Berkshire sees value in its own shares at current prices. A bullish signal from new management.
