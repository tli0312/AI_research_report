01 · Market Overview

What Is a Neocloud?

Neoclouds are purpose-built GPU cloud providers that emerged to fill the gap between hyperscalers' general-purpose compute and the raw GPU demand of AI model training and inference. Unlike AWS, Azure, and GCP — which layer AI services on top of diversified infrastructure — neoclouds deploy GPU-first, AI-only infrastructure: NVIDIA H100/H200 clusters, high-speed NVLink/InfiniBand networking, NVMe storage, and liquid cooling, all optimized for the parallel workloads that build and serve AI models.

The category emerged around 2020–2022 as frontier AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral) exhausted hyperscaler GPU availability and needed dedicated, contractually guaranteed compute at scale. Neoclouds stepped in with multi-year take-or-pay contracts, offering predictable capacity and dramatically lower per-GPU pricing driven by operational specialization and lower overhead.

Today approximately 200 providers claim the neocloud label, though the market is already bifurcating: CoreWeave operates at hyperscale ($5B+ annual revenue), a handful of Tier-2 players (Lambda, Nebius, Crusoe) sit at $250M–$1B run-rates, and a long tail of smaller GPU lenders is likely to consolidate or fail by 2026–2027.

🔼 Secular Tailwinds
  • ✦ Frontier model arms race — GPT-5, Gemini 3, Claude 4 training runs require massive dedicated clusters unavailable on hyperscalers at needed scale
  • ✦ Inference explosion — Agentic AI deployments are 10–100× more inference-intensive than chat; production serving demand compounding 80%+ annually
  • ✦ Sovereign AI — EU AI Act, GDPR, and national AI strategies driving demand for non-hyperscaler compute outside US jurisdictions
  • ✦ Hyperscaler GPU shortage — AWS, Azure, GCP still capacity-constrained for H100s; neoclouds offer faster provisioning with contractual guarantees
  • ✦ Price arbitrage — $34/hr vs $98/hr for equivalent H100; 65% savings drives enterprise migration for cost-sensitive workloads
🔽 Structural Headwinds
  • ▾ Hyperscaler price cuts — AWS cut H100 instance prices 45% mid-2025; hyperscalers have structural incentives to undercut
  • ▾ Proprietary silicon — Google TPU v5, AWS Trainium 2, Microsoft Maia threaten to make NVIDIA-based pricing irrelevant for captive workloads
  • ▾ GPU depreciation cycle — Hardware depreciates ~50% over 5 years; providers must recover capex in 48–60 months and immediately fund next-gen
  • ▾ Capital intensity — Post-depreciation gross margins of 14–16% leave minimal room for error; funded almost entirely by debt
  • ▾ Model efficiency gains — Architectural improvements (MoE, distillation, quantization) reduce compute required per inference dollar
🏗️ Barriers to Entry
  • ◆ Capital ($500M–$5B+) to build a competitive GPU cluster — access to NVIDIA allocation is itself gated by relationships and creditworthiness
  • ◆ Power & land — securing 100MW+ sites with power purchase agreements is a 12–24 month process in most markets
  • ◆ Networking expertise — InfiniBand / RoCE cluster design for 1,000+ GPU pods is operationally complex
  • ◆ Customer lock-in — multi-year take-or-pay contracts (CoreWeave: $66.8B backlog) create durable revenue but require trust and scale to sign
  • ◆ Not high at the software layer — neoclouds largely deliver raw compute; differentiation via software stack is still nascent

Deep Dives

CoreWeave CRWV
Nasdaq · Market Cap ~$43.6B · EV ~$70.3B
Public — IPO Mar 28, 2025

The dominant pure-play neocloud globally. CoreWeave provides specialized cloud infrastructure built on NVIDIA GPUs, targeting AI/ML training and inference workloads for hyperscalers, AI labs, and enterprise customers. Went public at $40/share on Nasdaq in March 2025 and has since traded up ~90%+ from IPO, reaching a ~$43.6B market cap despite persistent profitability concerns. Revenue exploded from $1.9B (2024) to $5.1B (2025), a 170% YoY increase — faster than any cloud platform in history.

The company's contracted backlog of $66.8B (as of Dec 31, 2025) provides extraordinary revenue visibility — approximately 10+ years at 2025 run-rates — anchored by OpenAI ($22.4B committed) and Meta ($14.2B committed through 2031). Microsoft was the dominant customer in 2024 (62% of revenue) but that concentration has shifted as the customer base diversified post-IPO.

⚠ Key Risk: ~$8.7B in total debt with ~$7.5B maturing by 2026. If credit markets tighten or risk premia spike, refinancing risk is significant. Adjusted EBITDA of $3.1B in 2025 provides some cover, but free cash flow remains deeply negative given capital deployment pace.

Valuation Framework

Neocloud valuation is a bet on three variables: (1) durability of the GPU price premium vs. hyperscalers, (2) contract renewal rates as multi-year take-or-pay agreements roll off, and (3) the debt refinancing cycle.

CoreWeave's 6.6–8.8x P/S looks reasonable against 170% revenue growth — on a PEG basis the multiple is actually below 0.1x. But traditional P/S analysis underweights the capital structure risk: the EV/Revenue of ~13.8x (including $26.7B in net debt) tells a different story, implying the market is pricing in sustained hypergrowth for 5+ years to justify current enterprise value.

Private comps (Lambda, Crusoe) are trading in private markets at significant premiums to public equivalents on a per-GPU or revenue multiple basis, suggesting the public market is actually conservative relative to late-stage private valuations.

Key Valuation Debates
  • 1. Backlog quality: CoreWeave's $66.8B backlog contains take-or-pay provisions, but customer health (especially AI labs that may run out of funding) is opaque. OpenAI and Meta anchor contracts are solid; the tail is less certain.
  • 2. EBITDA vs. FCF: Adjusted EBITDA of $3.1B masks ~$10–12B in annual capex. True cash economics won't be visible until the company slows GPU fleet expansion — likely 2027 at earliest.
  • 3. GPU obsolescence: H100 clusters being built today may face pricing pressure when NVIDIA Blackwell and future Rubin architectures become available at scale (2026–2027). Will neoclouds successfully pass hardware upgrade costs to customers?
  • 4. Comps migration: As CRWV matures, does it get valued as a cloud infrastructure company (5–8x revenue) or as a high-growth software-adjacent platform (12–20x)? The answer drives a $20–40B range of fair value outcomes.
05 · Investment Implications
↑ Bull Case
  • AI training compute demand is structurally undersupplied for 3–5 years; neoclouds are the fastest path to GPU capacity at scale
  • CoreWeave's $66.8B contracted backlog provides 10+ years of revenue visibility — extraordinary for any tech company
  • Inference workloads are growing 80%+ annually and will sustain demand even as training efficiency improves
  • Sovereign AI tailwinds create durable international demand outside hyperscaler reach
  • CoreWeave's 170% revenue growth with improving EBITDA margins shows operating leverage is real and accelerating
  • The sector is early — neoclouds are ~1% of the $3T+ cloud computing TAM; the growth runway is enormous
↓ Bear Case
  • Hyperscaler price cuts (AWS –45% on H100 mid-2025) signal aggressive defense of enterprise AI workloads
  • CoreWeave's debt wall (~$7.5B due 2026) is an existential refinancing risk if credit spreads widen
  • Customer concentration: losing OpenAI or Meta would be catastrophic; both have incentives to build in-house
  • GPU depreciation math is brutal: 14–16% post-depreciation gross margins mean any revenue miss destroys equity value
  • Model efficiency gains (DeepSeek R1-style architectures) reduce compute per token — demand could disappoint
  • ~200 neocloud providers today means massive sector shakeout coming; only 5–10 will survive at scale

Risk Matrix

● High Risk
Debt Refinancing Cliff
~$7.5B maturing by 2026 for CoreWeave. If spreads widen 200bp+, refinancing cost spikes materially. Market is currently pricing benign credit conditions.
● High Risk
Customer Concentration
2+ customers represent 50%+ of revenue at most neoclouds. Loss of anchor tenant or hyperscaler insourcing decision would structurally impair the business.
● Medium Risk
Hyperscaler Undercutting
AWS, Azure, GCP have capital, relationships, and proprietary silicon to commoditize GPU cloud. Timeline uncertain — likely 2–3 years before material impact.
● Medium Risk
GPU Obsolescence
NVIDIA Blackwell (B100/B200) and Rubin (2026+) architecture transitions could strand H100 investment or force premature fleet refresh.
● Medium Risk
Compute Efficiency Shock
DeepSeek-style architectural improvements could compress training/inference compute requirements faster than demand can absorb, creating GPU oversupply.
● Lower Risk
Regulatory / Export Controls
US chip export restrictions constrain international competition but could also limit neocloud expansion in Asia Pacific markets. Net slightly positive for US-based players.

Key Catalysts to Watch

Near-Term CoreWeave Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 earnings — Revenue beat vs. consensus, backlog expansion, and any update on debt refinancing terms will be the primary near-term stock driver for the sector
Near-Term Lambda Labs / Crusoe IPO filings — Expected 2026; would expand the public neocloud investable universe and provide fresh valuation benchmarks across the tier-2 cohort
Mid-Term Hyperscaler H100/H200 pricing moves — Any further price reductions from AWS, Azure, or GCP would pressure neocloud margins and force competitive response; monitor quarterly AWS pricing sheets
Mid-Term NVIDIA Blackwell availability at scale — When B100/B200 systems reach mass availability (H2 2025 – H1 2026), watch for neocloud fleet upgrade announcements and any resulting capex guidance changes
Mid-Term OpenAI / Anthropic contract renewals — CoreWeave's OpenAI commitment runs through 2030; any renegotiation signals would be significant. Anthropic's Fluidstack partnership is a comp to watch
Long-Term Sector consolidation — With ~200 neoclouds and only 5–10 viable at scale, M&A activity will accelerate. Acquirers likely include CoreWeave, hyperscalers, and well-funded privates looking to acquire power assets or customer relationships
Long-Term Inference infrastructure buildout — The shift from training to production inference at scale is the next step-change demand driver. Neoclouds that build inference-optimized infrastructure (low-latency serving, disaggregated memory) will capture the next wave
06 · Thematic Investment Angles
📈 Pure-Play Exposure

CoreWeave (CRWV) is the only publicly traded, scaled pure-play neocloud. For investors wanting direct exposure to AI infrastructure buildout without NVIDIA hardware dependency, CRWV is the primary instrument. High volatility, levered to AI capex cycle.

🌍 Geographic Diversification

Nebius (NBIS) offers European neocloud exposure with sovereign AI tailwinds. Smaller cap, higher execution risk, but addressable market is structurally underserved. EU AI Act compliance requirements favor non-US providers.

⚡ Infrastructure Picks-and-Shovels

Applied Digital (APLD) and IREN (IREN) offer leveraged power infrastructure plays with lower direct AI execution risk. Ex-Bitcoin miners pivoting to HPC/AI with existing power contracts — option value on infrastructure at lower multiples.
